What we offer

Programs designed for real reentry.

Our services focus on stability, skills, and supportive relationships during the most critical transition period.

Core programs

Support that meets people where they are.

Each program is designed to address common barriers faced during reentry while honoring individual goals and circumstances.

Reentry Navigation

Practical guidance for housing, identification, transportation, and employment readiness.

Mentorship Program

One-to-one mentorship relationships that provide accountability, encouragement, and connection.

Life Skills & Planning

Goal setting, budgeting basics, communication skills, and planning for long-term stability.

How it works

Clear steps. Consistent support.

While every participant’s journey is unique, our programs follow a clear and supportive structure.

  1. Initial connection
    Referral, application, or direct outreach to begin the process.
  2. Assessment & planning
    Identify immediate needs, goals, and available resources.
  3. Program participation
    Engagement in mentorship, skill-building, and coordinated support.
  4. Ongoing follow-up
    Continued connection and accountability as stability increases.

Who we serve

Individuals returning home.

We serve people transitioning from incarceration who are seeking stability, support, and direction. Participation may be based on availability, referrals, and program capacity.

  • Recently released individuals
  • People preparing for reentry
  • Individuals seeking mentorship and guidance
  • Families navigating reentry alongside loved ones
